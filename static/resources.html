Understanding Depression

  • Persistent sadness, loss of interest, or hopelessness lasting more than two weeks may indicate depression.
  • It is a medical condition, not a personal weakness — and it's treatable.
  • Talking to a licensed therapist or counselor is one of the most effective first steps.

Understanding Anxiety

  • Excessive worry, restlessness, or difficulty relaxing over an extended period can indicate an anxiety disorder.
  • Grounding techniques (like the 5-4-3-2-1 method) can help manage acute anxiety in the moment.
  • Regular exercise and reduced caffeine intake often help reduce baseline anxiety levels.

Everyday Self-Care Habits

  • Keep a consistent sleep schedule — aim for 7-9 hours nightly.
  • Stay physically active, even a short daily walk helps.
  • Maintain social connections — isolation tends to worsen most mental health conditions.
  • Limit alcohol and avoid using substances to cope with difficult emotions.
  • Practice mindfulness, journaling, or breathing exercises during stressful periods.

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Symptoms are interfering with work, school, or relationships.
  • You've had thoughts of self-harm or feel unable to cope.
  • Symptoms have lasted more than two weeks without improvement.
  • Reaching out to a professional is a sign of strength, not weakness.
